Alta X (Freefly Alta X quadcopter)
- equipped w wind sensors, temp sensors, humidity sensors
- measures micrometeorology over fires

Currently no FAA or NASA drone is capable of both soil data collection + wind data. For a drone to do both it needs following instrumentation:
- Anemometer: measures wind speed and direction
- Radiosonde: measures wind speed, humidity, temp, pressure
- IMU+GPS: measures ground velocity vs air velocity {use when no sensor available/hardware malfunction}
- Multispectral Camera: measures vegetation reflectance and estimates fuel moisture indirectly (5-15% error) AND/OR thermal camera that infers soil moisture via wet soil=cooler infrared 
- could use microwave/radar sensors: measure microwave emission from vegetation to achieve accuracy of up to 2-5% error. But tech is heavy and expensive
